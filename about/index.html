About

I’ve lived in the South and East Bays (California), Shanghai & Suzhou (China), Exeter (New Hampshire). Currently I’m living in Champaign (Illinois)!

Miscellaneous Interests

My first year of undergrad, I was involved in Model United Nations (UCBMUN), helping to run a national collegiate conference as well as an emergency response historical committee. I was also involved with Berkeley ANova, which is a student organization that seeks to empower students by providing computer science education and mentorship to under-resourced schools and marginalized communities. I co-chaired the events committee that started their large-scale overnight hackathon for high school students in under-resourced communities for two years.

For all four years, I was also an avid participant in the Blockchain@Berkeley community, developing curriculum and teaching courses, mentoring other students and managing projects and doing technical ghost-writing and advising different start-ups. Currently, I contribute to Bitcoin and do technical ghost-writing for various blockchain start-ups.

In my free time, I also like to deviate from recipes, sing along to songs I don’t know the words to, occasionally go on long runs, and ponder other hypothetical careers like stand-up comedy. In the past, I’ve run the Golden Gate Half (Fall 2016), and the Bay Bridge Half (Spr 2019).
